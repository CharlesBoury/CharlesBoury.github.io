I'm a game artist with eight years of experience.
I make interactive pictures to expose complex rules and dream of a better future.
I worked with different teams on award-winning games, and shipped half a dozen finished products.

What I can bring to your team

My core strength is graphic production with photoshop and blender, and lively scene setting in Unity. I craft polished, detailed animated screens as well as quick game mockups. I can set and follow an artistic direction that will be beautiful and memorable.

I have a lot of experience with designing and illustrating educational video games, finding an effective mix between fun and content. I see videogames as an invaluable tool to explore complex systems. I have an immense respect for the human that will play my games, and therefore spent time looking at how to best explain something. I bring informed point of view to the design team on gameplay, UX, and tone.

As an amateur programmer, I speak their language. I know how to make a game, down to the compiler. As a consequence, I understand what the console log is saying, I see through the matrix of text-serialized Unity scenes, and I know how to tame git. I take care of my tools, and am eager to help others. I have been in the position of technical artist in several teams. I'm pleased when there's generative coding or hand-written shaders on the menu.

What I'm looking for

I'm looking for fulfilling visual design work where I can be part of a small, fun team creating interactive experiences. I don't want to be too prescriptive about what that might be, be here are some things I seek.

I'm happiest when...

  • working on projects that genuinely help people and society
  • working on site, fulltime, with a small, technically competent team
  • collaborating with a caring, inclusive group of people
  • working iteratively to get to the intended goal
  • contributing to the why and what we're making
  • crafting effective gameplay and excellence in visuals

As for style and aesthetic, mine is versatile and subjected to the project, but I thrive when there's a contemplative world to give birth to.

Availability

I'm currently booked until spring, working for Ikigai as their Artistic Director, creating educational games at Sorbonne university.
I'm always interested to hear of new opportunities, so please get in touch! I'm very much up for meetings and chats over coffee about possible projects.
